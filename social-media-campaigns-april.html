Campaign Strategy

April is Financial Literacy Month. Schools are buzzing with money lessons, parents are Googling "how to teach kids about money," and educators are hunting for curriculum-ready resources. We ride that wave with a 4-week campaign that's equal parts wit, warmth, and urgency.

The Big Idea

"Every kid deserves a money moment." — The campaign positions Clarence as THE book of Financial Literacy Month: the one that sneaks real money skills into a story kids actually fight over.

4-Week Arc

  • Week 1 (Apr 1–7) — THE HOOK: "It's Financial Literacy Month — and nobody told the kids." Awareness, intrigue, bold claims.
  • Week 2 (Apr 8–14) — THE PROOF: Reviews, educator endorsements, real classroom moments. Social proof heavy.
  • Week 3 (Apr 15–21) — THE EDUCATION: Actual money concepts from the book. Value-driven, shareable content parents and teachers screenshot.
  • Week 4 (Apr 22–30) — THE CLOSE: Urgency, gift-giving angle, bulk orders, "don't let the month end without this book."

Audience Targeting

  • Primary — Parents (Ages 28–45): Parents of K–5 kids. Interested in education, parenting, children's books, financial literacy.
  • Secondary — Educators: K–5 teachers, curriculum coordinators, school librarians. Target with educator-specific content.
  • Tertiary — Gift-Givers: Grandparents, aunts/uncles. "Better than another toy" messaging.

Voice & Tone

  • Witty but warm. Think: the cool teacher who makes you laugh AND learn.
  • Confident, not salesy. We have a 5-star book and award-winning endorsements. Let them speak.
  • Slightly irreverent. "No coupons were harmed." "Recovering coupon addict." Lean into it.
  • Always purposeful. Every post either entertains, educates, or converts. Ideally two of three.

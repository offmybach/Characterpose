🛒
Module 1 • 15 Minutes
The Want vs. Need Showdown
Pages 1–12 ▼
📖 The Read

Read pages 1–12 aloud. Stop when Clarence realizes he doesn’t have enough money.

✋ The Activity — The Thumbs-Up Test

Call out items one at a time. Students give a thumbs-up for a NEED and a thumbs-down for a WANT.

🧥Winter Coat
🎮PlayStation
🥦Groceries
🍬Candy
🏠A Home
💡 The Lesson

“A budget prioritizes the thumbs-up items before the thumbs-down items. Needs come first — always.”

💡 Teacher Tip: Let students debate edge cases. Is a phone a need or a want? It depends on context — that’s the point.
🏷️
Module 2 • 15 Minutes
The Bargain Cartel
Pages 15–24 ▼
📖 The Read

Read pages 15–24. Stop when Clarence uses his coupon.

✋ The Activity — The Math

Write a price on the board. Tell students they have a coupon. Walk them through finding the new price.

$5.00
10% OFF
= $4.50
$10.00
25% OFF
= $7.50
$20.00
50% OFF
= $10.00
💡 The Lesson

“The price on the sticker isn’t always the price you pay. Smart shoppers do the math.”

💡 Teacher Tip: Start with 50% — kids get halving. Then 25% (half of half). Build to 10% last.
🐷
Module 3 • 15 Minutes
The Payoff
Pages 25–36 ▼
📖 The Read

Read to the end of the book.

✋ The Activity — My Savings Goal

Every student writes down one thing they want, how much it costs, and how many weeks of allowance or chores it would take to buy it.

I want to save for: e.g., A new skateboard
It costs: e.g., $45.00
I can save per week: e.g., $5.00
🧮 Weeks to goal = Cost ÷ Weekly savings
💡 The Lesson

“Saving isn’t punishment — it’s a plan. The earlier you start, the sooner you get what you want.”

💡 Teacher Tip: Connect to the 529 account from the book. Ask: What if a grown-up was saving for YOUR college right now?
